Boomtown


Boomtown is a data visualization that demonstrates the relationship between home values across Seattle over time and the corresponding stock prices of a few prominent Seattle area companies over the same timespan.


Skills Developed: Data cleaning, Tableau, User-Centered Design

Team size: 5

My Role: Product Management, Data Analytics

Tools used: Tableau

Problem


In the past few years, Seattle has witnessed a growth in the real-estate prices. It's been said that the rise of tech industry in Seattle has led to an increase in the real-estate prices. How is the rise of tech companies in Seattle related to the rise in real-estate price?

Design Solutions

After a lot of brainstorming, my teammates and I came up with varied ideas on how we can show the house pricings in Seattle area.


  • The idea behind this design was to filter based on a list of requirements such as neighborhood, schools, parks, hospitals etc. The user lists her preferences using a filter and those localities are listed along with the house prices over the last 5 years and the current year.

  • The idea behind this visualization is to compare stock price of Seattle corporation vs average home sales price in Seattle. Observe major events that may have impacted the housing market.
  • The viewer can hover over an event to read about event details and how it affected housing market.
  • The viewer will be able to overview the trend of housing price vs stock price of local corporations.

  • The goal of this visualization is to give home buyers the ability to see which neighborhood they can afford.
  • Inform home buyers on the crime and housing market status.

Data Collection

  • Our housing price data was provided by Zillow. The data set contains the median home value(ratio) for 132 zip codes in Seattle area.
  • The data is provided on a month to month basis from June 1996 to March 2019. This is about 18000 data points.
  • The stock price data was pulled from a dataset in Kaggle.com

Data Cleaning


We did some filtering in Tableau to exlude some unnecessary rows and columns in our datasets. Because Boomtown focuses on housing prices in the Seattle area only, we first checked the range of zip codes in Seattle and then filtered houses that are not in Seattle area.

Usability Testing

  • Participants did not understand the relationship between the stock charts and the housing price charts.
  • Neighborhood locations were not easily deciphered.

Design changes and iterations

Based on the feedback from our usability testing, we made the following changes in our design:

  • Changed our color palette from an orange-green color gradient to orange-blue to avoid being problematic for people with color blindness.
  • Updated the visualizationâ€™s labeling to clarify that the data shown in the map is one year later than the selection on the timeline.
  • The main elements of the visualization were re-organized to position the timeline above the map. This was in response to feedback that this new structure made it easier to notice and use the timeline to explore the data set.
